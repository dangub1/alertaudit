What's in the report

  • Real hit rate — every alert scored against actual market prices at its timestamp, no cherry-picking, no "we told you so" screenshots.

Method, in one paragraph

Every alert is matched to real historical market data at its timestamp. Entries and exits follow the channel's own words — when an alert names no exit, we apply a disclosed default rule and flag it. Nothing is excluded, weighted, or smoothed. The full methodology appears on page one of every report, so you (or your guru) can dispute any line.

Questions

Which markets do you cover?

US stocks and major crypto (spot) are fully supported. Options alerts are audited best-effort — directional wins/losses are scored precisely; exact contract P&L depends on how completely strikes and expiries were stated in the alerts, and anything ambiguous is flagged rather than guessed.

What exactly do I need to send?

The last 90 days of the channel's alerts (or at least ~15 of them) in any format that shows the original timestamps — screenshots, copy-pasted messages, a chat export, or a TradingView alert-log export. Timestamps must be the platform's own, not typed in afterward. Don't clean anything up: vague alerts, missing exits and deleted-then-reposted calls are part of the audit — we count them, and that count is often the most useful finding. If too little is scoreable, the report says exactly that, and if we can't parse your material at all, you get a refund.

Can you audit a TradingView indicator?

Yes — through its fired-alert log. Alerts fire in real time and can't be redrawn, so they're immune to repainting (scripts that redraw past signals to look prophetic). Export or screenshot your alert log — 90 days or 15+ alerts — and it's scored like any channel. We audit what verifiably fired, not what the chart repainted. Historical markers painted on a chart are not accepted as evidence.

Will the channel know I did this?

No. Your alerts are used only to produce your report, then deleted on request. We never publish the channel's name or your name. What you do with your report is up to you.

What if my alerts can't be parsed?

Full refund, no questions. If more than 30% of alerts are too vague to score, we'll tell you that too — and it's usually the most useful thing you'll learn about the channel.

Is this financial advice?

No. AlertAudit is an independent statistical analysis of a message history, for educational purposes. We don't recommend trades, channels, or strategies, and we're not affiliated with any signal provider.
