This sample was produced on the channel's public feed to demonstrate the method. The channel's name is withheld here; in a customer report, the audited channel is the one you subscribe to, named in full.

Methodology — identical in every report

  1. Source material. Every public post in the window was collected and counted. Nothing was excluded. The full post census is summarized in Section 4 and itemized in Appendix A.
  2. Scoring rule. A post is a scoreable advance signal if it names an instrument, a direction, an entry level, and was posted before the move it describes. Exits follow the signal's own targets/stop-loss.
  3. Price data. Entries, targets and stops are matched against 1-hour exchange OHLC data starting at the post's timestamp (no look-ahead). Sources in Appendix B, including a second-exchange cross-check.
  4. The $10K test. A simulated $10,000 account splits equally across all scoreable signals, entered per the disclosed entry rule, no leverage. Benchmark: holding BTC over the same window.
  5. Conservative tie-break. If a candle touches both a target and the stop, the stop is counted first.
  6. Independence. AlertAudit has no relationship with any signal provider. This is statistical analysis of a public message history, for educational purposes — not financial advice.

5. What this does and doesn't tell you

A 90-day window is a sample, not a verdict — and here the scoreable sample is two trades. Two wins out of two is consistent with genuine skill and with luck; the honest range (34%–100%) is printed in Section 1. Both wins came in small-cap alt-coins during a falling market, which is genuinely notable — but with n=2, no confident statement about skill is possible. What the data does support confidently: the channel's public feed is 0.5% actionable signal and 4% unverifiable performance marketing by volume, and its advertised accuracy figures cannot be checked against anything it has publicly posted. If you are paying for the VIP tier on the strength of those figures, you are paying for numbers that the public record cannot confirm.
